## 3Ô∏è‚É£ Components of Matplotlib

### Figure

The **figure** is the outer container that holds the entire chart or multiple charts.

### Axes

Axes represent the actual plotting area where data is drawn. A figure can contain one or more axes.

### Axis

Axis refers to the X-axis and Y-axis that define the scale of data.

### Labels & Title

* Labels describe what each axis represents
* Title explains what the graph is about

## 5Ô∏è‚É£ Line Chart

Line charts show **continuous data** and are mainly used to display **trends over time**.

### Characteristics:

* Data points connected by lines
* Best for time-series data
* Easy to compare growth or decline

### Use cases:

* Stock prices over time
* Temperature changes
* Sales trends

---

## 6Ô∏è‚É£ Bar Chart

Bar charts are used to **compare values across categories**.

### Characteristics:

* Rectangular bars
* Can be vertical or horizontal

### Use cases:

* Student marks comparison
* Product sales comparison
* Population by category

## 7Ô∏è‚É£ Pie Chart

Pie charts show **percentage or proportional data**.

### Characteristics:

### Use cases:

* Budget distribution
* Market share analysis
* Survey results
